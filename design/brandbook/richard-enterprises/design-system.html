Design Principles

01

Institutional Restraint

Every surface earns its place. No decoration without function. The holding company speaks through absence of noise — charcoal backgrounds, minimal borders, maximum data density.

02

Gold as Signal

Gold is never decorative. It marks: primary actions, active states, financial values, and hierarchy anchors. One gold element per viewport focus area. Restraint amplifies prestige.

03

Navy Authority

Navy conveys institutional depth — secondary actions, data containers, active navigation. The Wayne side of the DNA. Paired with gold for the full brand signature.

04

Entity-First Architecture

Every component answers: what entity is this, what is its status, and where does it sit in the hierarchy? Portfolio cards, entity trees, investment summaries — structure mirrors corporate structure.

05

Glass Depth

Frosted glass surfaces layer information without hiding context. The substrate is visible beneath every container. Blur 12-40px, gold-tinted borders at low opacity.

06

Three-Generation Scalability

Components must serve today's three subsidiaries and tomorrow's twenty. The grid system, card patterns, and hierarchy displays scale from family office to institutional PE without redesign.

Dashboard Composition

Tier 1: Stat Bar

4-column grid of StatCards at top of dashboard. Portfolio revenue, employee count, active instruments, deployment %. First thing the board sees.

Tier 2: Portfolio Grid

PortfolioCards in auto-fill grid (340px min). One per subsidiary/entity. Tabs above to filter by type. This is the operational overview.

Tier 3: Detail Panel

EntityHierarchy + InvestmentSummary + MeetingCards in sidebar or below-fold. Deep drill-down context. Triggered by selecting a PortfolioCard.
