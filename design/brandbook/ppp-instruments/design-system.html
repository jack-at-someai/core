Design Principles

01

Provenance as Architecture

Every visual traces back to ownership. The provenance timeline is not a feature but the structural spine of every instrument view. Maker, owner, player, dealer, certifier — each is a node in the chain.

02

Burgundy, Gold, Cream

Burgundy for authority and fund identity. Gold for value and appreciation signals. Cream for warmth and historical resonance. A palette that could hang in a concert hall or a boardroom.

03

Category Color Mapping

Violin = red. Bow = blue. Cello = purple. Viola = green. Every instrument category has a fixed color that carries across cards, charts, timelines, and portfolio views.

04

Maker Tier Hierarchy

Tier 1 (Stradivari, Guarneri, Amati) = gold border. Tier 2 (Vuillaume, Pressenda, Steiner) = mauve border. Tier 3 = neutral border. The maker badge tells you the investment tier at a glance.

05

Appreciation is the Story

Every instrument exists on a curve. The design must make appreciation trajectories feel inevitable and patient. Long arcs, not spikes. Decades, not quarters.

06

Glass Morphism for Depth

Frosted surfaces let the fund's depth show through. Portfolio layers feel stacked, not flat. The instrument is always the focal object; the data is the translucent frame around it.
