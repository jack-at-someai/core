Design Principles

01

The Vault Door

Dark backgrounds are the display case. Every card, every price, every grade sits on deep black. The vault frames the collection. Vault Black (#0A0A0A) is the dominant surface across all views.

02

Championship Gold

Gold is reserved for value signals: prices, primary CTAs, active states, and premium indicators. Seven rings, one accent color. Gold draws the eye to what matters — the money, the grade, the action.

03

Grade-First Hierarchy

Every card display shows grade before anything else. PSA 10 Gem Mint in emerald green. The grading color scale (Gem through Poor) is the most critical semantic system in the entire UI.

04

Rarity as Identity

Mythic (pink glow), Rare (purple), Uncommon (blue), Common (slate). Rarity badges are never optional. The rarity system borrows from TCG conventions (Magic: The Gathering, Pokemon) and extends to sports card scarcity.

05

Display Typography

Oswald for impact: prices, headings, brand marks. Inter for clarity: body text, metadata, navigation. The two-font system separates the showroom from the back office.

06

Experiential Retail

The digital experience mirrors the physical vault: dark walls, glass cases, gold lighting, curated displays. Components have physical weight — cards lift on hover, cases have depth shadows, prices glow.

Page Composition

Storefront View

NavBar + SearchBar + Tabs (category filter) + TCGCardDisplay grid (auto-fill 200px min). This is the main browsing experience. Cards lift on hover. Infinite scroll or pagination at bottom.

Inventory Manager

NavBar + SearchBar + filter selects (sport, grade, rarity) + InventoryRow list. Compact view for staff. Clicking a row opens detail panel with PriceComparisonBar and full card data.

Locations Dashboard

Stat cards row (total revenue, total inventory, locations count) + StoreLocationCard grid (340px min) + map integration. Performance comparison across all retail locations.
