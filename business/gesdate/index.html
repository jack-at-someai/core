The Problem

Every farm is an island

Swine gestation is 114 days — 3 months, 3 weeks, 3 days. Miss a breeding date, you miss a farrowing. Miss a farrowing, you lose a litter. Across the industry, the tools for tracking this are remarkably primitive.

  • Paper calendars and whiteboards — the most common gestation tracking tool in American barns
  • Spreadsheets that die with laptops — years of breeding data lost to hard drive failures and forgotten passwords
  • No cross-herd analytics — a breeder has no idea how their litter sizes compare to the breed average
  • No breed-level performance data — which sires throw larger litters? Nobody can answer that across operations
  • Fringe software that doesn't talk — GesDate, EasyPig, PigCHAMP, handwritten notebooks, and Facebook Messenger threads

Vision

The entry point

GesDate is the entry point. Every breeding cycle recorded is a fact in Charlotte's graph. Every farm that joins extends the network. Every sire that appears in two different operations creates a bridge between herds that never knew they were connected.

The goal: predict genetic outcomes before the breeding happens. Not guess — predict. With enough data across enough operations, Charlotte can tell you what a particular boar-sow pairing is likely to produce before a single piglet is born. Litter size. Birth weight distribution. Growth trajectory. Breed-level tendencies corrected for individual variation.
